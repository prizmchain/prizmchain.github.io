Privacy Policy

QuietLog - Noise Recorder & Decibel Report

Last Updated: June 20, 2026

Privacy First - Our Commitment

QuietLog is designed as a local-first noise recording and decibel report app.

  • No account sign-up is required
  • No advertising SDKs are used
  • No third-party analytics SDKs are used
  • Your noise sessions, audio recordings, detected events, reports, thresholds, and settings are stored locally on your device
  • Microphone access is used only to measure sound levels and save session audio when you start a recording
  • Purchase status is processed by Apple and RevenueCat so QuietLog Pro can be unlocked and restored

About Audio Recordings

QuietLog can save raw audio and over-threshold noise event clips locally on your device.

  • QuietLog does not upload your recordings, decibel timeline, reports, or event clips to our servers
  • Audio may contain surrounding sounds or voices depending on where you record
  • You are responsible for using recording features in compliance with local laws and obtaining any required consent
  • You can delete reports inside the app or remove app data by uninstalling the app
  • You can revoke microphone permission at any time in iOS Settings

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

We explicitly do not collect:

  • Name, email address, or phone number
  • Account credentials or user profiles
  • Precise location data or GPS location
  • Contacts or address book
  • Health or fitness data
  • Advertising identifiers for tracking
  • Cross-app tracking data
  • Analytics profiles or behavioral advertising data
  • Your recordings, reports, or decibel history on our servers

3. Data Storage

3.1 Local Device Storage

QuietLog stores app data locally on your device. This may include:

  • Noise session history and report summaries
  • Decibel timeline samples and detected noise events
  • Raw audio recordings and event clips created inside the app
  • Report names, location labels, threshold values, and session duration settings
  • Free report state, Pro access state, and purchase restoration cache used for app functionality

3.2 Microphone and Background Audio

QuietLog uses the microphone when you start a noise recording session. If background audio is enabled by the system, QuietLog may continue recording while the app is not visible so the active session can continue. This is used only for the recording session you start.

3.3 Reports, Sharing, and Export

QuietLog may let you view, play, share, or export reports and audio. When you choose to share or export a report or recording, that content leaves the app through the destination you select, such as Messages, Mail, Files, or another app. Those destinations are governed by their own privacy practices.

3.4 Data Retention

  • Local data remains on your device until you delete it in the app or uninstall the app
  • Deleting a report removes the report data controlled by QuietLog from local app storage
  • Uninstalling the app may remove locally stored QuietLog data from your device
  • Purchase records are handled by Apple and RevenueCat so purchases can be restored

4. Permissions We Request

Permission Why We Need It Required?
Microphone To measure sound levels and save session audio when you start a recording Required for recording
Background Audio To allow an active recording session to continue when supported by iOS Optional / system controlled

Note: You can manage microphone permission in iOS Settings -> QuietLog at any time.

5. How Your Data Is Used

Your data is used only to provide the app's core functionality:

  • Noise measurement: To measure sound levels and show dBA readings during a session
  • Event detection: To mark moments that exceed your selected threshold
  • Audio playback: To let you review the original recording or event clips inside reports
  • Reports: To summarize peak dBA, exceeded time, event count, and session details
  • Local history: To keep previous reports available on your device
  • Purchase information: To unlock or restore QuietLog Pro

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or cross-app tracking.

7. Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your data:

  • Local-first design: Recordings, reports, and noise history are stored on your device
  • iOS sandboxing: App data is protected by iOS app sandboxing
  • Secure purchases: Payments are processed by Apple through the App Store
  • No ad tracking: QuietLog does not integrate advertising trackers

No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure, but QuietLog is designed to minimize unnecessary data exposure.

8. Recording Responsibility and Measurement Notice

QuietLog is a practical noise recording and reporting tool. It is not a certified sound level meter and should not be treated as a substitute for professional acoustic measurement equipment. Audio recording laws vary by country, state, and region. You are responsible for using QuietLog legally and appropriately.

9. Children's Privacy

  • No public user profiles are created
